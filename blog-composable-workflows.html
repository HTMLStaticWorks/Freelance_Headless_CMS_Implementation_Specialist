Composable Workflows.

Designing seamless workflows between content teams and developers in a composable ecosystem.

In a monolith, the workflow is often rigid. In a composable architecture, you have the freedom to design workflows that match how your team actually works.

Bridging the Gap

The key to a successful headless implementation is not just the technology, but how people use it. Developers and content creators need to speak the same language.

Modular Content Components

Instead of fixed page templates, think in terms of reusable components. This allows content editors to build pages dynamically while developers maintain the component library.

Clear Governance

Define roles and permissions clearly within your CMS to ensure that content is reviewed and approved by the right people before going live.

Automation is Key

Use webhooks to trigger automated tests, preview builds, and notifications. This reduces friction and allows for faster iteration cycles.

Conclusion

Composable workflows empower teams to work in parallel, leading to faster time-to-market and a more agile organization.
